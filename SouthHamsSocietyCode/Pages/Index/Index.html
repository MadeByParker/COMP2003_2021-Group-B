South Hams Society

The South Hams Society is an environmental and heritage charity, founded in 1961, with the objective of protecting both the natural landscape and the built character of the South Hams and, in particular, the South Devon Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty. To do so, the Society works to encourage high standards of planning and architecture, respecting the special character of the area and fostering a healthy biodiversity. Staffed entirely by volunteers, the Society supports the right development in the right places, while actively opposing any inappropriate and harmful development.
Please explore this website to find out more about the work we do and how you can help us.

Collaton Park Letter of Objection

It’s hard to see how the proposed construction of 125 homes and a number of commercial business units close to Newton Ferrers won’t irreparably harm the landscape and scenic beauty of the AONB. The site sits exposed on high ground. As a result it will be highly visible from large areas of Heritage Coast in the location and add considerably to the light pollution in the night sky. The need for the development is also highly questionable. Just three miles away as the crow flies or five miles by road 5,500 new homes are currently under construction at Sherford, with a further 1,500 planned post 2026.
